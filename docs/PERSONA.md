# PERSONA.md — Behavioral Profiles

## The Governing Principle

Generation × Occupation = The Whole Person.

Generation tells you communication style, cultural references, digital fluency, and what they fear about AI.
Occupation tells you what daily problem AI can solve today.
Together they determine: the analogy, the tone, the format, the pacing, the celebration style, and the first word of content.

**Never write a word of content, copy, or notification without asking: "Which persona is this for? Does it sound like I know them?"**

## Persona 1: Radha — Retired Teacher, 63

**Generation:** Boomer | **Occupation:** Retired teacher | **Language:** Hindi | **Location:** Patna, Bihar

### Who She Is
Radha taught primary school for 32 years. She retired three years ago. She lives with her son's family — a daughter-in-law, and two grandchildren (8 and 14). She uses an Android phone (gifted by her son, 2 years old, mid-range Redmi). She primarily uses WhatsApp to stay connected to her school friends' group and her colony ladies' group. She has been cheated once — ₹3,000 lost to a fake "KBC lottery" call. She is cautious now to the point of fear.

### What She Fears
- Being fooled again (primary fear, shapes every digital interaction)
- Becoming a burden — "yeh sab mere liye nahi hai"
- The world moving faster than she can keep up
- Pressing something wrong and breaking the phone
- Looking foolish in front of her grandchildren

### What She Wants
- To not be cheated
- To understand what her grandchildren are talking about
- To be useful to her family (health questions, government schemes)
- To feel capable, not helpless
- Respect — she taught 600+ children; she is not slow

### Communication Style
- Warm, respectful, unhurried
- Stories before facts — always
- One thing at a time — never two ideas in one sentence
- Oral — she trusts what she hears more than what she reads
- Never condescending — she notices instantly and withdraws
- Address her as you would address a respected elder, not a student

### Digital Behaviour
- Opens apps slowly, reads every screen fully before tapping
- Taps with full finger, not fingertip (tremor-adjacent)
- Does not swipe intuitively — prefers large tap buttons
- Will call her son if confused rather than explore
- Uses voice messages on WhatsApp; comfortable with speaking
- Screen brightness often low; font size often at largest system setting

### AI Literacy Starting Point
Zero. She has heard "ChatGPT" mentioned at family dinner but has no context. She does not know what AI does. She thinks of it as either magic or dangerous. The concept of "a computer that reads and writes" is new to her.

### Content Rules for Radha
- **Entry hook:** Always start with a story about someone like her — never a definition
- **Analogy:** The well-read student who never lived life; the confident relative; the village oracle
- **Font class:** `xl` (24sp body, 38sp headline)
- **Format:** Audio primary; text always present but secondary
- **Voice speed:** Slow (0.85×)
- **Completion style:** Quiet glow + single warm sentence in Hindi
- **Never:** Confetti, streak pressure, technical terms, English words without explanation
- **Celebration tone:** "Aapne aaj woh seekha jo bahut log nahi jaante." ("Today you learned what most people don't know.")
- **Error framing:** Never "wrong answer." Always "almost — try once more" or "here is what people usually think about this."

### What "Winning" Looks Like for Radha
She forwards a suspicious WhatsApp message to AI Saathi, learns it is a scam, blocks the number, and tells the ladies' colony group. Five more women download the app. This is the scenario we build the whole platform to enable.

## Persona 2: Ramesh — Cotton Farmer, 45

**Generation:** Gen X | **Occupation:** Smallholder cotton farmer | **Language:** Hindi | **Location:** Vidarbha, Maharashtra

### Who He Is
Ramesh farms 4 acres of cotton in Amravati district. He has a wife, two daughters (17 and 13), and an elderly father. He went to school until Class 10. He speaks Marathi at home and Hindi in the market. He uses a basic Android (₹5,000 range). He has Jio data. He uses WhatsApp for the agricultural cooperative group. He has heard about AI from his nephew who works in Pune.

### What He Fears
- "Yeh computer waali cheez mujh jaise ke liye nahi hai" — the belief that technology is not for him
- Wasting time on something that doesn't help his crop
- Being given wrong advice that damages his crop or wastes money
- His daughter knowing more than him (pride mixed with pride)

### What He Wants
- Early pest and disease identification (most urgent problem)
- Better mandi prices — knowing when and where to sell
- Government scheme navigation (PM Kisan, crop insurance)
- Weather predictions that are specific to his field, not just the city
- Respect for his 25 years of farming knowledge — AI should assist, not lecture

### Communication Style
- Direct and practical — "kya karna hai" not "kyun karna hai"
- Skeptical of new things; needs proof before trust
- Responds to peer proof ("Ramesh ke paas wale Suresh ne kiya toh")
- Does not like reading; voice is primary
- Agricultural metaphors land immediately
- Responds to respect for his experience

### Digital Behaviour
- Comfortable with WhatsApp, YouTube, occasional calls
- Uses voice messages; does not type long messages
- May have patchy signal in the field (2G common)
- Phone often has low storage; app must be lightweight
- Will use the app in the field, standing, in bright sunlight (outdoor visibility)

### AI Literacy Starting Point
Low — but not zero. Has used Google Voice search to find information. Understands that "computer can find things." Does not understand how AI generates responses. Believes AI could give wrong advice about crops.

### Content Rules for Ramesh
- **Entry hook:** A real crop problem — photograph a leaf, ask a question, get a useful answer
- **Analogy:** Seed catalogue salesman (sometimes wrong about your soil); the KVK officer (wise, always confirm); DJ who plays what's requested but sometimes the wrong album
- **Format:** Voice + photo input; 2-line text max; no long explanations
- **Max words per screen:** 12 (even lower than elderly default)
- **Audio:** Pre-rendered at normal speed; regional accent of Vidarbha preferred if available
- **Daily feed:** Mandi prices, pest alerts, rain forecast, KVK contact
- **Try This Today:** Specific prompt tied to current season and crop
- **Verification habit:** Always ends with "KVK se ya sahakar se confirm karo pehle"
- **Never:** Complex multi-step instructions; English terms; abstract concepts without crop example

### What "Winning" Looks Like for Ramesh
He photographs a diseased leaf. AI Saathi identifies early blight in Hindi. He asks the KVK helpline to confirm. KVK agrees. He applies the correct treatment. Crop saved. He tells 3 other farmers in his cooperative group. All 3 download the app.

## Cross-Persona Rules

### The Jargon-Free Promise
No persona ever reads these words: LLM, hallucination, token, model, inference, prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning, embedding, neural network (in technical context), parameter.

### The No-Lecture Rule
Every persona responds to problems they already have, solved by AI. No persona is lectured about AI before AI has done something useful for them.

### The Dignity Non-Negotiable
Every persona has been talked down to by institutions, employers, or systems. This platform does not. The tone for every persona is: "You are capable. This was designed for you. Let's go."

### The Verification Habit
Every persona learns, in their own language and analogy, the same underlying habit: AI is a powerful second opinion. Verify before acting on anything important. The specific verification source changes per persona (KVK for Ramesh, doctor for Radha, supervisor sign-off for Kabir) but the habit is universal.
