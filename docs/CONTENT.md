# CONTENT.md — 3-Month Content Plan

## Content Architecture

Every concept is taught across **5 daily micro-cards** (Monday–Friday). A **weekly Good Read** drops on the same Sunday the new concept opens. **Streak bonus cards** trigger at milestones. The **daily feed** runs independently from live APIs.

One concept = 7 days:
- Day 1 (Mon): The Hook — story, no concept name yet
- Day 2 (Tue): The Reveal — what it actually means
- Day 3 (Wed): The Practice — try this now
- Day 4 (Thu): The Retrieval — campfire question
- Day 5 (Fri): The Check — did this help?
- Day 6 (Sat): Rest / community story
- Day 7 (Sun): Weekly Good Read drops + next concept's hook

#### Day 2 (Reveal) — What AI Can Now Do

**English:**
That call was made by an AI that cloned her son's voice. It had listened to 30 seconds of his voice from YouTube — and created a perfect copy.

This is new. Scam calls used to have bad grammar, strange accents, obvious mistakes. AI has removed all those clues.

**Three new signs to look for:**
1. URGENT + PRESSURE to act immediately — real emergencies allow you to pause
2. "Don't tell anyone" — real family doesn't say this
3. Asking for money via an unknown number — real banks and real family use their real numbers

**Hindi:**
वो call AI ने बनाई थी। उसने YouTube से बेटे की 30 second की आवाज़ सुनी — और perfect copy बना ली।

यह नया है। पहले scam calls में grammar गलत होती थी। अब AI ने वो सारे signs हटा दिए हैं।

**तीन नई पहचान:**
1. **URGENT** — बहुत जल्दी — असली emergency में थोड़ा रुकने का समय होता है
2. **"किसी को मत बताना"** — असली परिवार ऐसा नहीं कहता
3. **अनजान number से पैसे माँगना** — असली bank और परिवार अपने असली number से बात करते हैं

**Bengali:**
সেই কলটা AI বানিয়েছিল। ছেলের ৩০ সেকেন্ডের কণ্ঠস্বর শুনে — নিখুঁত নকল তৈরি করেছে।

এটা নতুন। আগে scam call-এ ভুল বাংলা থাকত। AI সেই সব চিহ্ন মুছে দিয়েছে।

**তিনটি নতুন চিহ্ন:**
1. **জরুরি** + **তাড়াতাড়ি** — আসল জরুরি অবস্থায় একটু থামার সময় থাকে
2. **"কাউকে বলো না"** — আসল পরিবার এটা বলে না
3. **অচেনা নম্বরে টাকা চাওয়া** — আসল bank এবং পরিবার নিজেদের আসল নম্বর থেকে কথা বলে

### Week 3–4: Concept 11 — Privacy (What Never Goes Into AI)

#### Day 1 Hook (Hindi)
एक कहानी: रमेश के पड़ोसी ने एक "government app" में अपना Aadhaar डाला। तीन हफ्ते बाद, उनके PM Kisan account से ₹6,000 गायब।

App fake था। Aadhaar real था।

**एक rule, हमेशा के लिए:**
Aadhaar नंबर, OTP, bank PIN — ये तीन चीज़ें कोई भी app, website, या AI को कभी नहीं बतानी।

#### Day 2 Reveal
Three things that never go into any AI — ever:
1. Aadhaar number
2. OTP (any OTP, from any source)
3. Bank account number, PIN, or password

Why: Public AI tools are not private vaults. What you type can be used to improve the AI. Even if it isn't — you don't know.

The rule is simple: If it's something you wouldn't shout across a busy market — don't type it into an AI.

#### Day 3 Practice
"AI Saathi me yeh kabhi mat type karo. Yeh raha ek safe sawaal aur ek unsafe sawaal — pehchaano."

Safe: "PM Kisan scheme mein kaun apply kar sakta hai?"
Unsafe: "Mera Aadhaar hai 1234-5678-9012. Kya mujhe PM Kisan milega?"

### Week 5–6: Concept 1 — AI is a Pattern Matcher

#### Day 1 Hook (Hindi)
एक बहुत पढ़ा-लिखा student था। उसने हर किताब पढ़ी थी — गणित, विज्ञान, इतिहास, साहित्य। लेकिन उसने कभी खेत नहीं देखा था। कभी बाज़ार नहीं गया था। कभी किसी बीमार बच्चे की देखभाल नहीं की थी।

आप उससे खेती के बारे में पूछते हैं — वो किताब से जवाब देता है। सही हो सकता है। गलत भी हो सकता है।

AI बिल्कुल वही है।

#### Day 1 Hook (Bengali)
একটা ছেলে আছে — সব বই পড়েছে। অঙ্ক, বিজ্ঞান, ইতিহাস। কিন্তু কখনো বাজারে যায়নি। খেত দেখেনি। অসুস্থ মানুষের সেবা করেনি।

তাকে চাষ সম্পর্কে জিজ্ঞেস করলে — বই থেকে উত্তর দেবে। ঠিক হতে পারে। ভুলও হতে পারে।

AI ঠিক এটাই।

#### Day 2 Reveal
AI is not magic. It is not a mind. It is the world's most well-read student who has never lived a day outside of what it has read.

It learned by reading: websites, books, conversations, news, research papers — billions of them.
It sees patterns in what it has read.
When you ask it something, it finds the best pattern match from everything it has read.

That is all it does. Pattern matching. Very fast. Very wide. But not thinking. Not feeling. Not knowing your specific situation unless you tell it.

The Blind Men and the Elephant: each man was perfectly right about what he touched — and completely wrong about the whole animal. AI is all six men working together, very fast. It still hasn't seen the whole elephant.

#### Day 3 Practice
"AI Saathi se poochho: 'Mujhe apne baare mein batao.'"

Notice: AI knows nothing about you. It answers generically. This is the point. Unless you tell it who you are, it cannot help you specifically. Tomorrow we learn how to fix this.

### Week 7–8: Concept 7 — AI Can Be Confidently Wrong

#### Day 1 Hook (Hindi)
घर में एक बड़े हैं — सब से राय लेते हैं। बहुत confidence से बोलते हैं। एक बार उन्होंने एक दवाई की गलत dose बता दी। Confident थे। गलत थे।

कोई जानबूझकर गलत नहीं बोला। बस उन्हें उस specific दवाई की सही जानकारी नहीं थी — और इसका उन्हें पता नहीं था।

AI बिल्कुल ऐसा ही करता है।

#### Day 2 Reveal
AI does not know when it is wrong. This is the most important thing to understand about AI.

A human expert usually knows what they don't know. AI doesn't have this self-awareness. It predicts the next most-likely word in a sequence — and that mechanism produces confident text regardless of accuracy.

The result: AI can state something completely false with perfect confidence, in perfect language, without any hesitation.

This is not a bug. It is how language models work. And it is why every important AI answer needs one verification step before you act on it.

**Persona-specific names for this:**
- Elderly (Radha): "The confident relative who gives medicine advice from memory"
- Farmer (Ramesh): "The seed salesman who didn't read the actual label"
- Gig worker (Priya): "GPS routing through a road that closed 3 months ago"
- Clerk (Kabir): "Junior colleague who drafts a note and quietly invents one fact"
- Student (Arjun): "Brilliant teammate who sometimes makes things up"
- Shop owner (Dilip): "Supplier who confidently quotes wrong stock availability"

#### Day 3 Practice — Spot the Wrong Answer
Show 3 AI answers about a familiar topic. One has a subtle error. User identifies it.

**For Radha:** Three AI answers about common medicines. One has a wrong dosage. She finds it.
**For Ramesh:** Three AI answers about pesticide spray timing. One says spray in afternoon (wrong — morning or evening). He finds it.
**For Arjun:** Three AI answers about a chemistry concept. One has a wrong formula. He finds it.

### Week 9–10: Concept 4 — The Djinn Principle (Precision Changes Everything)

#### Day 1 Hook (All personas)
एक कहानी है — जिन्न की।

एक किसान था। उसने जिन्न से माँगा: "मेरे खेत को इतना पानी दो कि हमेशा हरा रहे।"

जिन्न ने पूरा खेत पानी से भर दिया।

जिन्न ने गलत नहीं किया। उसने exactly वही किया जो माँगा गया था।

किसान ने सही नहीं माँगा।

AI भी जिन्न की तरह है।

#### Day 2 Reveal
AI gives you exactly what you ask for. Not what you meant. Not what you needed. What you asked.

"Mere patte ka kya?" → Generic answer about leaf problems
"Mere tamatar ke patte ke kinare peele ho rahe hain, October mein, Vidarbha mein, 2 din se" → Specific, useful, actionable answer

The more specific your question, the more useful the answer.

Five things that make any question better:
1. Who you are ("Main 65 saal ki hoon...")
2. What you have ("Mera tamatar ka khet hai...")
3. What happened ("2 din se patte peele ho rahe hain...")
4. Where you are ("Nashik district mein...")
5. What you want ("Kya main kuch kar sakti hoon aaj?")

### Week 12: Concept 12 — You Are In Control

#### Day 1 Hook (All)
Thor's hammer, Mjolnir — has a condition. Only the worthy can lift it.

AI has no such condition. Anyone can use it. But using it well — that requires something.

Judgement. Verification. Knowing when to trust and when to check. Knowing what not to share. Knowing when to say "this is not for AI — I need a real person."

**You are in control. AI works for you.**
